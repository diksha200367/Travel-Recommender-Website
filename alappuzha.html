Alappuzha

Venice of the East

Alappuzha, often referred to as the "Venice of the East," is a mesmerizing destination in Kerala known for its enchanting backwaters, serene beaches, and rich cultural heritage. This picturesque town is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and those looking for a peaceful retreat. One of the main attractions of Alappuzha is its stunning backwaters, where visitors can embark on a scenic houseboat cruise, drifting through palm-fringed canals while experiencing the charm of traditional village life. The famous Vembanad Lake, the longest lake in India, offers breathtaking views and a perfect setting for birdwatching and kayaking.
For beach lovers, Alappuzha Beach is a must-visit, with its golden sands, historic pier, and vibrant sunset views. The town is also home to the renowned Nehru Trophy Boat Race, an exhilarating annual event that showcases Kerala's traditional snake boat races. History enthusiasts can explore the majestic Krishnapuram Palace, a beautiful 18th-century structure adorned with stunning murals and ancient artifacts. The Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, located nearby, is a haven for birdwatchers, housing a variety of migratory and native bird species.
Beyond sightseeing, Alappuzha offers a delightful culinary journey with its authentic Kerala cuisine. Visitors can savor fresh seafood delicacies, traditional Sadya meals served on banana leaves, and the famous Karimeen Pollichathu, a flavorful grilled fish dish. For those seeking relaxation, the town is dotted with renowned Ayurvedic wellness centers offering rejuvenating treatments and massages. Shopping enthusiasts can explore the local markets, known for their handwoven coir products, spices, and handicrafts.
