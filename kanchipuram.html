Kanchipuram

The City of Thousand Temples

Kanchipuram, often called the City of a Thousand Temples, is a historic and spiritual hub in Tamil Nadu, India. Renowned for its magnificent temples built in the Dravidian architectural style, the city is a significant pilgrimage center for Hindus, particularly devotees of Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu. Iconic temples like the Ekambareswarar Temple, Kailasanathar Temple, and Varadharaja Perumal Temple showcase intricate carvings and ancient heritage. Apart from its religious importance, Kanchipuram is world-famous for its exquisite handwoven silk sarees, known for their rich zari work and fine craftsmanship. With a history dating back over 2,000 years, Kanchipuram was once a major center of learning and culture under the Pallava dynasty. The city continues to attract visitors with its blend of spirituality, history, and traditional artistry.
