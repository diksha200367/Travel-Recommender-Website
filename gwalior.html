Gwalior

A city in Madhya Pradesh

Gwalior, a historic city in Madhya Pradesh, is renowned for its magnificent forts, royal palaces, and rich cultural heritage. Dominated by the grand Gwalior Fort, often called the "Gibraltar of India," the city has been a witness to centuries of history and valor. Gwalior is home to architectural marvels like the Sas Bahu Temples, Jai Vilas Palace, and the Tomb of Tansen, the legendary musician of Emperor Akbar’s court. The city is also famous for its contribution to Indian classical music, hosting the prestigious Tansen Music Festival annually. With its blend of history, music, and stunning architecture, Gwalior offers a captivating glimpse into India’s glorious past while retaining its old-world charm.
