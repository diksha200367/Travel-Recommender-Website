Indore

A city in Madhya Pradesh

Indore, the commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh, is a vibrant city known for its rich history, booming industries, and delectable street food. Blending old-world charm with modern development, it has been consistently ranked as Indiaâ€™s cleanest city and is a major educational and business hub. The city's heritage is reflected in landmarks like Rajwada Palace, a stunning blend of Maratha, Mughal, and French architectural styles, and Lal Bagh Palace, which offers a glimpse into the royal past of the Holkar dynasty. Religious sites like Khajrana Ganesh Temple and Annapurna Temple attract devotees from across the country. Indore is a paradise for food lovers, with Sarafa Bazaar and Chappan Dukan offering an irresistible variety of snacks like poha, jalebi, and Indori samosas. The city also serves as a gateway to the famous pilgrimage site of Ujjain and the hill station of Mandu. With its thriving business sector, educational institutions like IIT and IIM, and a lively cultural scene, Indore is a dynamic city that perfectly balances tradition and progress.
