Hampi

A city in Karnataka

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, is a mesmerizing landscape of ancient ruins, massive boulders, and lush greenery. Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, it is now a treasure trove of history and architecture, attracting travelers, history enthusiasts, and backpackers from around the world. The city is home to breathtaking temples like the Virupaksha Temple, one of the oldest functioning temples in India, and the Vittala Temple, famous for its stone chariot and musical pillars. The Tungabhadra River adds to the charm of Hampi, with coracle rides offering a unique way to explore its scenic beauty. Hampi's rocky terrain is perfect for adventure seekers who enjoy bouldering, while its surreal landscape provides a peaceful retreat for those looking to unwind. With its blend of history, spirituality, and natural beauty, Hampi offers a truly enchanting experience that takes visitors back in time.
