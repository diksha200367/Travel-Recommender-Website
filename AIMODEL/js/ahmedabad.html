Ahmedabad

The Cultural Hub of Gujarat

Located in Gujarat, Ahmedabad is a city wherehistory, culture, and modernity come together in perfect harmony. Known as the Manchester of India, it has a rich industrial history, but it’s also deeply connected to the Indian independence movement due to the Sabarmati Ashram, the home of Mahatma Gandhi. The city is a melting pot of Gujarati culture, reflected in its traditional pols (housing clusters) and heritage walks through narrow lanes, where you can admire old architectural marvels. The Jama Masjid, Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, and Bhadra Fort are prime examples of the city’s historical grandeur. Ahmedabad is also famous for its stunning Sarkhej Roza complex and the Adalaj Stepwell, which are architectural wonders. The city thrives on its rich food culture—its street food scene, especially dishes like dhokla, khandvi, and famous Gujarati thali, is legendary. The Navratri Festival, celebrated with great enthusiasm, is one of the largest dance festivals in India. Whether you're exploring the old city or enjoying the modern amenities of the Sabarmati Riverfront, Ahmedabad offers a captivating journey through time and culture.
