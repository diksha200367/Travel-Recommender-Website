Coorg

The Scotland of India

Coorg, officially known as Kodagu, is a breathtaking hill station in Karnataka, often referred to as the "Scotland of India" due to its misty landscapes and lush greenery. Nestled in the Western Ghats, this serene destination is famous for its coffee plantations, dense forests, and cascading waterfalls.
The region is home to the majestic Abbey Falls and Iruppu Falls, which offer stunning views, especially during the monsoon season. Trekking enthusiasts can explore the scenic trails of Tadiandamol, the highest peak in Coorg, or take a nature walk through Nagarhole National Park, which is rich in wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and deer.
Coorg is also famous for its rich Kodava culture, with traditions that include unique warrior dances and delicious cuisine. Visitors must try traditional Kodava dishes like Pandi Curry (pork curry) and freshly brewed Coorg coffee, renowned for its strong aroma and flavor.
