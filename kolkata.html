Kolkata

Cultural Capital Of India

Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, is a city rich in history, culture, and intellectual heritage. Known as the "Cultural Capital of India," it was once the capital of British India and retains its colonial charm through landmarks like Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, and St. Paul’s Cathedral. The city is a hub of literature, art, and cinema, home to legends like Rabindranath Tagore and Satyajit Ray. Its vibrant festivals, especially Durga Puja, showcase its deep-rooted traditions, transforming the city into a grand celebration of art and devotion. The city's tram system, bustling College Street, and historic Park Street add to its old-world charm, while the Eden Gardens remains a paradise for cricket lovers. Kolkata’s markets, such as New Market and Gariahat, are a shopper’s delight, offering everything from traditional Bengali sarees to street food delicacies. The city's famous Bengali cuisine—including rasgulla, mishti doi, kathi rolls, and kosha mangsho—makes it a food lover’s paradise. With its blend of heritage, modernity, warmth, and artistic spirit, Kolkata offers a unique and unforgettable experience to all who visit.
