Banaras

The City of Bhakti

Varanasi (Banaras) is one of the world’s oldest living cities and a sacred Hindu pilgrimage site. Situated on the banks of the Ganges, it is famous for its ghats, where devotees perform rituals, offer prayers, and witness mesmerizing Ganga Aarti, a spiritual spectacle of fire-lit lamps and chants that illuminate the night. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, holds immense religious significance, drawing millions of devotees each year. Just outside the city, Sarnath is where Buddha delivered his first sermon, making it a key site for Buddhist pilgrims. The city's narrow lanes, bustling markets, and spiritual essence offer a unique experience that captures the essence of India’s deep-rooted traditions. Boat rides on the Ganges at sunrise provide breathtaking views of the ancient city awakening. The city's rich heritage is also reflected in its classical music, renowned Banarasi silk sarees, and delicious street food, such as kachoris and malaiyo. Varanasi is a city where spirituality and culture converge, creating an unforgettable journey for visitors.
