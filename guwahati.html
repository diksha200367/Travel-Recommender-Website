Guwahati

A city in Assam

Guwahati, the gateway to Northeast India, is a vibrant city nestled along the banks of the Brahmaputra River in Assam. Known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient temples, and scenic landscapes, Guwahati seamlessly blends tradition with modernity. The city is home to the revered **Kamakhya Temple**, one of the most significant Shakti Peethas, attracting devotees from all over the country. Other notable attractions include the **Umananda Island**, the worldâ€™s smallest inhabited river island, and the **Assam State Zoo**, which houses diverse wildlife. Guwahati also serves as a hub for exploring the enchanting tea gardens of Assam and the nearby Kaziranga National Park, famous for its one-horned rhinoceroses. With its bustling markets, serene river cruises, and delicious Assamese cuisine, Guwahati offers a unique experience that beautifully captures the essence of Northeast India.
